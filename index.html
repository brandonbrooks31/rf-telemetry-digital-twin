Metric Deep Dive: Why These Vectors?
We don't just look at single sensor spikes. We correlate 6 dimensions of RF physics to identify the "DNA" of a failing part. Here is why we chose these metrics and how they verify part integrity against proven ITU-R and RTCA datasets.

Power & Signal Quality

RSSI (Received Signal Strength Indicator): Measures the raw power of the incoming transponder signal. Sudden drops are often "Cable Loss Canaries"—indicating water ingress in coaxial cables or loose TNC connectors.

SNR (Signal-to-Noise Ratio): The ultimate measure of receiver health. A degrading SNR at constant RSSI predicts LNA (Low Noise Amplifier) thermal runaway long before the part fails airworthiness checks.

Data & Spectral Integrity

BER (Bit Error Rate): Measures the percentage of bits that were flipped during transmission. High BER at nominal power is a signature of Multipath Interference or Bit-Flip Anomalies in the digital backend.

Carrier Deviation: Measured in parts per million (ppm). If the local oscillator drifts beyond ITU-R M.1177 standards, the aircraft risks being "invisible" to Secondary Surveillance Radar (SSR).

Spatial & Energy Metrics

Doppler Shift: Captures the frequency offset relative to velocity. Mismatches between GPS-reported speed and RF-measured Doppler identify FMS (Flight Management System) integration lag.

Tx Power Stability: High-frequency monitoring of the Power Amplifier's output envelope. "Jitter" in Tx power is the primary indicator of failing SSPA (Solid State Power Amplifier) stages.

Verification Against Proven Datasets

The Mahalanobis Distance calculation isn't arbitrary. It creates a multi-dimensional "ellipsoid of normality" based on three proven data pillars:

  • RTCA DO-260B Compliance: We seed the model with the minimum performance standards required for FAA certification.
  • Tail-Specific Physics Baselines: Not all aircraft are equal. We "bootstrap" a unique baseline for every tail number during Phase I.
  • ITU-R M.1177 Spectral Masking: Our anomaly thresholds correspond directly to international radio regulation limits.

When a part drifts outside this multi-physics boundary, S(x) spikes. We catch the failure when it is a "Soft Fault" — saving the cost of a "Hard Failure" AOG.
